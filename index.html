Electricity & Circuits Intuition Roadmap

This page is a curated roadmap for learning electricity and circuits deeply through intuition, experiments and thought experiments. Instead of only memorizing formulas, the resources here focus on understanding why circuits behave the way they do.

Suggested Learning Order

  1. Play with circuits in Falstad simulator
  2. MIT 6.002 Circuits and Electronics
  3. MIT 8.02 Electricity and Magnetism
  4. The Art of Electronics
  5. Learning the Art of Electronics (hands-on)
  6. AlphaPhoenix & Ben Eater videos
  7. Feynman Lectures on Physics

Interactive Tools

Falstad Circuit Simulator

One of the best tools for developing circuit intuition. You can build circuits and literally see current flow and voltages update in real time.

Open Simulator

LTspice

A professional circuit simulator used by engineers. Excellent for testing real analog circuits, amplifiers and transient behavior.

Core Courses

MIT 6.002 – Circuits and Electronics

MIT's foundational electrical engineering course. It teaches abstraction, Kirchhoff laws, nodal analysis and circuit reasoning.

Course Page

MIT 8.02 – Electricity and Magnetism

Explains the physics behind circuits: electric fields, potentials, and electromagnetic energy flow.

Course Page

Books

The Art of Electronics

Considered the best electronics book ever written. Instead of only theory it explains how circuits actually behave in the real world and how engineers think when designing them.

Learning the Art of Electronics

A hands-on lab book with experiments that build intuition by actually building circuits and measuring behavior.

The Feynman Lectures on Physics

Richard Feynman's legendary lectures give deep conceptual understanding of electromagnetism and energy flow in circuits.

Great Video Channels

AlphaPhoenix

Fantastic visual explanations and thought experiments about electricity and electronics.

Ben Eater

Deep explanations of digital electronics, signals and computer hardware built from scratch.

Walter Lewin MIT Lectures

Legendary MIT physics lectures famous for dramatic demonstrations of electromagnetic phenomena.
