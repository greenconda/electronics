Electricity & Circuit Intuition Learning Path

This page collects some of the best courses, books and tools for developing deep intuition about electricity and electronic circuits. The focus is not just learning formulas but understanding why circuits behave the way they do through thought experiments, physical reasoning and practical demonstrations.

Recommended Learning Order

  1. Falstad Circuit Simulator – Play with circuits visually.
  2. MIT 6.002 Circuits and Electronics – Core circuit reasoning.
  3. MIT 8.02 Electricity and Magnetism – Physical intuition behind electricity.
  4. The Art of Electronics – Practical electronics intuition.
  5. Learning the Art of Electronics – Hands‑on lab experiments.
  6. AlphaPhoenix & Ben Eater – Real circuit experiments and explanations.
  7. Feynman Lectures on Physics – Deep conceptual understanding of electromagnetism.

Interactive Tools

Falstad Circuit Simulator

Open Simulator

A browser-based circuit simulator that visually shows voltage, current flow and component behaviour in real time. It is excellent for building intuition because you can modify circuits and instantly see how current redistributes. Many students find that experimenting here makes Kirchhoff's laws and parallel/series networks suddenly make sense.

LTspice

Download

A professional analog circuit simulator widely used by engineers. While more technical than Falstad, it allows deeper exploration of real electronic components and behaviours such as transient responses, amplifier stability and power electronics.

Core Courses

MIT 6.002 – Circuits and Electronics

Course Page

This is MIT's foundational electrical engineering course. It teaches how engineers abstract physical systems into circuit models and analyze them using tools such as Kirchhoff's laws, nodal analysis and Thevenin equivalents. The course focuses heavily on developing intuition through reasoning and sanity checks rather than blindly applying formulas.

MIT 8.02 – Electricity and Magnetism

Course Page

This physics course explains the fundamental phenomena behind circuits: electric fields, potentials, current flow and electromagnetic energy. Understanding these ideas provides deeper insight into why circuit laws work.

Books

The Art of Electronics

This classic electronics book teaches practical circuit design and real‑world engineering intuition. Instead of focusing purely on theory, it emphasizes understanding behaviour of components, approximations engineers make and how circuits actually behave in practice.

Learning the Art of Electronics

A laboratory companion that provides hands‑on experiments. By building circuits physically, students develop much stronger intuition about voltage, current distribution, noise, component limitations and measurement techniques.

The Feynman Lectures on Physics

Richard Feynman's famous lectures provide deep conceptual explanations of electromagnetism and energy flow in circuits. They help bridge the gap between circuit equations and the physical processes happening in electromagnetic fields.

Video Channels

AlphaPhoenix

A YouTube channel known for visual demonstrations and clever thought experiments about electricity. It often explains topics like Ohm's law and current flow in intuitive ways that challenge common misconceptions.

Ben Eater

Focused on building electronics and computer systems from scratch. His videos provide excellent intuition about digital electronics, signals and how hardware actually works.

Walter Lewin MIT Lectures

These famous MIT physics lectures feature dramatic demonstrations of electromagnetic phenomena and help students visualize electric and magnetic fields.
